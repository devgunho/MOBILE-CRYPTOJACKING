Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get started?

Linux plans start with the ability to run one job simultaneously without parallelism at no charge. Open source projects get 3 additional free containers, so you can use the power of parallelism and/or run more than one job concurrently. Purchasing a Linux plan enables access to additional containers at $50/container/month.

During the signup process, you can decide which plan(s) you need—you can build for free regardless!

How do containers work?

Every time you push to your VCS system, we checkout your code and run your build inside of a fresh, on-demand, and isolated Linux container pre-loaded with most popular languages, tools, and framework. CircleCI, in many cases, will detect and automatically download and cache your dependencies, and you can fully script any steps or integrations. Learn more about containers

What is concurrency? What is parallelism?

Concurrency refers to utilizing multiple containers to run multiple jobs at the same time. Otherwise, if you don't have enough free containers available, your jobs queue up until other jobs finish.

Parallelism splits a given job's tests across multiple containers, allowing you to dramatically speed up your test suite. This enables your developers to finish even the most test-intensive jobs in a fraction of the time.

How many containers do I need?

Most of our customers tend to use about 2-3 containers per full-time developer. Every team is different, however, and we're happy to set you up with a trial to help you figure out how many works best for you. As your team grows and/or as the speed of your build grows you can scale to any number of containers at any level of parallelism and concurrency is right for your team.

Why should I trust CircleCI with my code?

Security is one of our top priorities. Read our security policy to learn more about why many other customers rely on CircleCI to keep their code safe.

Can I change my plan at a later time? Can I cancel anytime?

Yes and yes! We offer in-app tools to fully control your plan and have account managers and an international support team standing by when you need help.

What if I want something custom?

Most of our customers find that our fully scriptable build configuration and dependency caching gives them broad leeway in customizing their build. If you require a self-hosted option or have specialized needs for your build fleet contact us; we're here to help teams of all sizes ship better code, faster.

What if I am building open-source?

We offer a total of four free linux containers ($2400 annual value) for open-source projects. Simply keeping your project public will enable this for you!

We also offer the Seed plan free (at 1x concurrency) for OS X open source projects. Contact us at billing@circleci.com for access. If you are building a bigger open-source project and need more resources, let us know how we can help you!

Why would I choose an macOS plan (as opposed to a Linux Plan)?

If you develop for Apple-related software (e.g., you are an iOS developer), you will likely need an macOS plan to ensure your tests run on our secure, private cloud of OS X machines.

OS X plans start with a two-week trial with access to our Growth Plan (5x concurrent jobs). At the end of your two-week trial, you may choose the plan that fits your needs best.

Linux plans allow customers to build on multiple isolated Linux systems. All customers get 1 free linux container and then Linux plans offer access to additional containers available at $50/container.

What plan do I need?

Weâve provided recommendations based on the ranges of builds but every team is different regarding their frequency and length of builds—as well as their need for engineer support.

To solve for this, everyone starts with a free 2-week trial at the Growth Plan which will help you determine all of those factors. We are happy to help you work through which plan is right for your team.

What is concurrency?

Concurrency refers to running multiple jobs at the same time (e.g., with 2x concurrency, two jobs triggered at the same time will both kick off, but on 1x concurrency one job will queue, waiting for resources).

Concurrency avoids slow-downs in work as your builds may otherwise queue behind the builds of someone else on your team.
