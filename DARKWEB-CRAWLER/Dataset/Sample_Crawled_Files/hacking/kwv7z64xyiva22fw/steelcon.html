We wanted to make the event free for everyone but, unfortunately, we have limited space and are worried that people would grab tickets just because they could, so we decided we had to charge. Entry price is just Â£25 and, likeÂ previous years, in return for your money you’ll get a goody bag with all sort of cool stuff. These are some of the thingsÂ we’ve given out so far:

We have a similar plan for this year, another bag full of fun stuff which will be paid for by your entry fee.

Cancellation Policy

Very important, we do not refund ticket money. All the ticket money goes towards buying the goody bag contents and so we start to spend it as soon as it starts to come in. If you contact us a week before asking for your money back, we no longer have it so can’t refund it.

The best thing to do is to pass the ticket on. We don’t mind if you resell it or give it away, if you want to change names on it then get in touch and we can do that otherwise just tell the new owner to turn up and pretend to be you, we don’t do ID checks but we do only allow one person per ticket.

If you decide to keep the ticket because you want the goody back, we do not post them out. You can nominate someone to pick it up for you on the day but make sure they do it early as well sell off all spare bags in the afternoon.

Friday Night

You are here: Home » Friday Night

Friday night is an informal get together in the Roebuck TavernÂ from around 7PM. Drinks are on you or find a friendly face with a company credit card and expense account (listen out for the Southerners, they tend to be more generous than us Northerners).

Last year we shared the pub with a wedding party but still had plenty of space and managed to take over the pool room and front patio area and will be trying to do the same again this year. We are easy to spot and a friendly bunch, even if you are feeling shy, come and say hi, you’ll soon fit in.

Bring and Buy Stall

You are here: Home » The Event » Bring and Buy Stall

In 2015 we did a bring and buy stall for used tech books with all the proceeds going to charity. It went well and we raised a few quid so in 2016 and 2017 we opened it up to hardware as well as books, this went very well and raised even more money and so it is obvious that we need to bring it back in 2018.

Bring along any old books, hardware, tech or anything else you no longer need but think someone else in the hacker community would enjoy. If your donation is unusual, or you think it requires some explanation as to what it is, then please stick a note on it.

All donations can be dropped off on Saturday morning at registration or earlier in the week during training if you need to but please get in touch to warn us before hand so we have somewhere to store it.

Parking

You are here: Home » Parking

As a lot of you will be driving in I’ve just done some quick car park research.

In my expertly designed map, the yellow box is the conference area.

Box 1 is theÂ NCP Furnival Gate, this is the closest reasonable priced car park. Be aware though, the gates are locked at midnight so if you are a non-drinker staying for the Saturday night party, this is not for you.

Box 2 is also reasonable priced but a little further to walk. This is ran by APCOAÂ and has 24/7 access.

Box 3 is NCP Arundel Gate, avoid this at all costs! Probably due to it’s proximity to the theatres, it is over 4 times as expensive as Furnival Gate on a Saturday and at least double the rest of the time.

Box 4 is a Q Park locally known as the Cheese Grater, it isn’t as expensive as Arundel Gate but still more than the other two.

There are also a number of council and charity ran car parks around the area but most are pay and display and you’ll end up running out to feed the meter every few hours.

For anyone coming in on Friday evening, the on street parking is about Â£1 from around 18:30 and free from about 20:00.
